# User Research Synthesis
## Key Themes
- **Lack of billing transparency**: Users cannot connect their energy consumption to bill amounts
- **Trust and accountability gap**: Energy companies are perceived as opaque, creating frustration and skepticism
- **Desire for actionable insights**: Users want to understand *how* to reduce costs, not just see what they owe

## Core User Pain Points
- Bills arrive without clear breakdowns of usage by time period, appliance, or rate tier
- Users suspect they're being overcharged but lack evidence to challenge bills or identify savings opportunities
- No visibility into how rates are calculated or why bills fluctuate month-to-month

## Actionable Recommendations
- Develop granular, easy-to-read bill breakdowns showing hourly/daily usage patterns and associated costs
- Create in-app or online dashboards with comparative insights (e.g., "Your usage 15% above similar homes")
- Implement proactive alerts when usage spikes, paired with specific reduction tips
- Consider simplified rate explanations or educational content embedded in billing communications

## Confidence & Caveats
This synthesis reflects a clear, consistent pain point across the sample, though we lack details on user demographics, bill complexity, or whether some users have already adopted monitoring tools. Follow-up research should explore what specific bill format changes would drive comprehension and whether users trust energy company-provided insights or prefer third-party data.
